About Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand, nestled in the lap of the Himalayas, is known as the "Devbhumi" or Land of the Gods. This northern Indian state is a paradise for nature lovers and spiritual seekers. With snow-capped peaks, lush valleys, sacred rivers like the Ganga and Yamuna, and ancient temples, Uttarakhand offers a blend of adventure, peace, and devotion.

Major attractions include hill stations like Nainital and Mussoorie, pilgrimage sites such as Kedarnath, Badrinath, Haridwar, and Rishikesh, and national parks like Jim Corbett for wildlife enthusiasts. Whether it's trekking in the Himalayas, rafting in the Ganges, or meditating by the riverside, Uttarakhand has something for everyone.

Top Travel Tips for Uttarakhand

  • Carry warm clothes, even in summer — weather changes quickly in the hills.
  • Respect local culture and religious sites — remove shoes before entering temples.
  • Start mountain travel early in the day to avoid landslide-prone routes after dark.
  • Always carry a reusable water bottle to stay hydrated and reduce plastic waste.
  • Try local Garhwali and Kumaoni cuisine — especially Aloo Ke Gutke and Bal Mithai!
